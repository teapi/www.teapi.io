Lists

A list is a named collections of documents associated with a type. For example, you might have a list named newest associated with the type books, which would likely represent a list of newly added books.

Lists are designed to cover a number of common cases, such as providing a list of recently changed or updated documents, as well as being useful for currated content. For example, you might have a list of favorites or onsale.

Strictly speaking, list aren't necessary. Whatever you can achieve with a list, you can also achieve with an index. An index on the created_at field would let you see a list of recently added books (via the sort=-created_at filter). A category field could let you flag some content as being onsale or a favorite.

Despite this redundancy, lists have a few advantages. First, they'll perform better than an index and take less space. They also have their own API, allowing you to push items onto the list and automatically purging older records. Finally, they're accessible via a friendly endpoint: /TYPE/LIST_NAME and automatically sort from most recently added to least-recently added.
